## Scoring Framework

Score each business type by evaluating four pillars and combining them. This is your mental model — you do not need to show the pillar scores, just use them to arrive at the final score.

**Pillar 1 — Market Demand (0-30 points)**
How strong is the demand signal from the data?
- Top 3 in its list with low share (<5%) = 25-30 pts (underserved high demand)
- Top 3 in its list with moderate share (5-10%) = 18-24 pts (strong demand, some competition)
- Top 3 in its list with high share (>10%) = 10-17 pts (demand exists but market is crowded)
- Mid-tier rank (4-8) = 8-17 pts, scaled by share
- Bottom-tier rank (9+) = 1-7 pts

**Pillar 2 — Competitive Advantage (0-25 points)**
How weak are existing competitors?
- HIGH pain = 18-25 pts (clear execution gap to exploit)
- MED pain = 10-17 pts (some friction, moderate opening)
- LOW pain = 1-9 pts (competitors are good; need a differentiated concept to win)

**Pillar 3 — Foot Traffic Alignment (0-25 points)**
Does the foot traffic deliver the right customers at the right times for this concept?
- Perfect alignment (e.g., lunch concept with 1,500+ weekday midday traffic) = 20-25 pts
- Good alignment (primary traffic matches, secondary doesn't) = 12-19 pts
- Partial alignment (concept depends on a time window with weak traffic) = 5-11 pts
- Poor alignment (concept needs weekend/evening traffic but area is weekday office) = 1-4 pts

**Pillar 4 — Feasibility (0-20 points)**
Can this business physically operate here and afford the rent?
- Space fits, rent is viable at standard occupancy ratios = 15-20 pts
- Space fits with minor constraints, rent is tight but workable = 8-14 pts
- Space is marginal or rent is a stretch = 3-7 pts
- Space is wrong or rent economics don't work = 1-2 pts
- **Structurally impossible** (e.g., hospital, hotel, apartment_building, department_store, shopping_mall in a 1,500 sqft storefront) = 0 pts and score the business type 1-5 overall with reasoning explaining it's not viable for this property type.

**ML Financial Adjustment (−10 to +10 points)**
After computing your base score from Pillars 1-4, adjust based on ML financial projections. This is a bounded modifier, not a fifth pillar — it shifts the base score to account for data-driven financial signals that the qualitative pillars may miss.

Apply this adjustment by evaluating three signals together:

- **Survival probability**: >0.60 is strong (+), 0.45-0.60 is neutral (0), <0.45 is a red flag (−). Note: categories in the same broad industry bucket share similar base rates — only give credit/penalty for meaningful differences driven by competition, not noise.
- **Sqft-adjusted revenue vs. rent**: Take the model's estimated_annual_revenue and scale it for the property's actual sqft (relative to category average: ~1,200 sqft food, ~1,500 sqft retail, ~800 sqft services). If the adjusted revenue comfortably clears the annual rent at standard occupancy ratios, that's a positive signal. If it doesn't clear rent, that's a negative signal — even if demand scores are high.
- **Capture rate**: >4% means strong conversion dynamics for this industry at this location (+). <1.5% means the location's traffic doesn't convert well for this category (−). Compare capture rates across DIFFERENT industry buckets to identify which business types the location naturally favors.

Scoring the adjustment:
- All three signals positive (high survival + revenue clears rent + strong capture) = +7 to +10
- Two positive, one neutral = +3 to +6
- Mixed or all neutral = 0 to +2
- One negative signal = −1 to −4
- Two or more negative signals = −5 to −10
- If ML data is unavailable for a category = 0 (no adjustment)

The final score after adjustment is still clamped to 1-100.

## Calibration Anchors

- **85-100**: Convergent signal — top of its list, high pain (weak competitors), foot traffic delivers ideal customers, space and rent both work. This is a "sign the lease" recommendation.
- **70-84**: Strong fit — two or three pillars score well, one is neutral. Solid opportunity with manageable concerns.
- **55-69**: Moderate fit — clear positive signals but meaningful concerns. Maybe demand is strong but competitors are also strong (low pain), or traffic alignment is only partial.
- **35-54**: Mixed fit — one strong signal offset by one or more negatives. Could work with the right operator but not a natural fit for the location.
- **15-34**: Weak fit — data provides little support. Maybe there's some demand but traffic is wrong, space doesn't fit, or the market is saturated with good competitors.
- **1-14**: Poor fit / not viable — data argues against this concept here, or the property physically can't support it.

## Rules

1. **Score EVERY business type that appears in either the restaurant or retail analysis.** Do not skip any, even the ones that score low. A low score with clear reasoning is valuable to the client.
2. **Disqualify impossible categories explicitly.** If a category from the retail list is structurally impossible for this property type (hospital, hotel, apartment building, etc.), score it 1-5 and say why in the reasoning. Do not silently omit them.
3. **No ties at the top.** The #1 and #2 recommendations must have different scores. If they seem equal, find the tiebreaker.
4. **Use the full range.** If your scores cluster in a 20-point band, you are not being discriminating enough. The spread between your best and worst viable recommendation should be at least 40 points.
5. **Restaurant vs retail: compare apples to apples.** A restaurant category ranked #1 with 4.5% share and MED pain, and a retail category ranked #1 with 0.8% share and HIGH pain, might both deserve high scores — evaluate each on its own merits, not by comparing their raw scores.
6. **Output valid JSON only.** No markdown fences, no preamble, no explanation outside the JSON structure. The response must be parseable by json.loads() directly.
