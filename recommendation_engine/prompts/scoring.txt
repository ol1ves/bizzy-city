Using the reasoning above and the raw market data, produce a final scored list of business type recommendations for this property.

Output a JSON object with a single key "recommendations" containing an array of objects. Each object must have:
- "business_type" (string): A clean, human-readable name. Use title case with spaces (e.g., "Mexican Restaurant", "Coffee Shop", "Acai Shop"), not snake_case or code-style names.
- "score" (integer, 1 to 100): How good a fit this business type is for this property.
- "reasoning" (string): 1-3 sentences explaining the score. Reference specific data points — scores, market share percentages, pain levels, foot traffic numbers.

## Scoring Calibration

Use the full 1-100 range. Here are anchors:

- **80-100**: Strong signal from multiple data sources. High demand, weak competition, foot traffic aligns well, space fits the concept.
- **60-79**: Good signal from at least one data source. No major red flags. Reasonable fit.
- **40-59**: Mixed signals. Some positive indicators but notable concerns (e.g., strong demand but poor traffic alignment, or good fit but saturated market).
- **20-39**: Weak fit. Maybe one positive signal but several negatives.
- **1-19**: Poor fit. The data actively argues against this business type at this location.

## Rules

1. **Be comprehensive.** Score EVERY business type that appears in either the restaurant analysis or retail analysis. Do not cherry-pick only the good ones. A low score with clear reasoning is valuable.
2. **No ties at the top.** If two business types are close, pick a winner and explain why one edges the other out.
3. **Differentiate.** Spread scores across the range. If everything clusters between 60-75, you are not being discriminating enough.
4. **Output valid JSON only.** No markdown fences, no preamble, no explanation outside the JSON structure. The response must be parseable by json.loads() directly.
